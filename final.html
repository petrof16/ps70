Final Project

Final Project: I hacked an old 25 key keyboard to harvesting electricity by turning the kinetic energy from the pressing of the keys, into voltage. I hot glued 12mm piezos to the bottom of each key so that when the keys were pressed, a small amount of voltage would be created by the piezos. The atoms in the crystals within the piezos are squished together and then return to their previous physical location. This slight moving of the electrons is what creates the electricity. Since the electrical energy generated by the piezos is AC, I had to convert the flow into DC. At first, I used bridge rectifiers made out of four diodes on a bread board but for the final version of the project, I used a rectifier on each piezo, which was soldered onto a proto board. Rather than using a single capacitor, I split the keyboard up into 4 sections, each with its own capacitor. The bottom 2 capacitors (1 & 2 on the LCD) had a higher capacity than the other, which is why you see that capacitors 3 and 4 filled up to 3.3 volts much faster than capacitors 1 & 2.
