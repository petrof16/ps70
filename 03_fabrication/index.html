<br> #### Week 3: Hand Tools and Fabrication I am making a cowbell playing kinetic sculpture. <img src="../images/platform.jpeg" alt="platform" width="484" height="363"> I screwed a metal rod onto a wooden platform. Next, I cut two wooden square rods to the same length using the band saw. <img src="../images/cowbell.jpeg" alt="cowbell" width="484" height="363"> I then drilled a single hole in each of the wooden sticks so that I may thread a thin metal rod through them that would provide the fulcrum. I put some tape on the thin metal rod between the two wooden, square sticks so that they would remain the correct length apart. <img src="../images/metal.jpeg" alt="motor" width="484" height="363"> In order to screw the motor onto the wooden platform, I drilled two holes onto a thin metal sheet and bent it so that the motor would reside between the cowbell and the fulcrum. Inspired by Leonardo da Vinci's Power hammer, I modelled two small cams using fusion 360. <img src="../images/davinci.jpeg" alt="davinci" width="640" height="640"> One would have a horizontal hole for the motor arm, and the other, a vertical hole to fit over the motor arm. <img src="../images/horizontal.jpeg" alt="horizontal" width="144" height="144"> <img src="../images/vertical.jpeg" alt="vertical" width="144" height="144"> <img src="../images/cams.jpeg" alt="cams" width="484" height="363"> Six equal-height feet were glued onto the bottom of the platform to provide space for the screws extruding fromt the bottom. I assume it will also help with the sound production.
